Understanding the Herding Effect

The Herding Effect in financial markets refers to the phenomenon where individuals tend to follow the actions of a larger group, even if their private information suggests a different course of action. This can lead to market inefficiencies, increased volatility, and even bubbles or crashes.

Our agent-based simulator provides a powerful tool to investigate the mechanisms behind herding behavior. By modeling individual agents with varying degrees of rationality, information access, and social influence, we can observe how collective behaviors emerge and impact market prices.
